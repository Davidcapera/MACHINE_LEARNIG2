Case Use Overview

Title: Air Quality Prediction Using Decision Tree

Problem Description

Air pollution is one of the most significant environmental challenges affecting human health, urban ecosystems, and climate conditions worldwide. High concentrations of particulate matter, especially PM2.5 and PM10, can lead to respiratory diseases, cardiovascular problems, and reduced quality of life. Traditional air monitoring systems rely on complex sensor networks and expensive infrastructure. This case demonstrates how a Machine Learning model can simplify this process by using only two measurable variables to classify air quality levels efficiently.

Supervised Learning Classification Decision Tree
Input Features
  • PM2.5: Fine particles that penetrate lungs
  • PM10: Larger particles affecting air quality
Expected Output
Good Moderate Unhealthy Hazardous
Real-World Relevance

Air quality prediction plays a key role in modern society by enabling smarter decisions in public health, urban planning, and environmental protection. It helps authorities issue early warnings, supports smart city systems, and allows individuals to take preventive measures against pollution exposure. This demonstrates how even simple Machine Learning models can provide valuable insights in real-world applications.
