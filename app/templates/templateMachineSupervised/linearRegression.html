Title

Sales Prediction from Advertising Investment

Problem Description

A company wants to understand which advertising channels drive the most sales so the marketing team can allocate budgets efficiently and forecast revenue. We apply Multiple Linear Regression to model the relationship between advertising spend in thousands of dollars across TV, Radio and Newspaper, and the resulting sales volume in thousands of units. The learned equation allows predictions for any combination of budget inputs.

Type of Machine Learning

Supervised Learning Regression Linear Regression

Input Features

1

TV ($K)

TV advertising budget per market. Range: 0.7 – 296.4 $K

2

Radio ($K)

Radio advertising budget per market. Range: 0.0 – 49.6 $K

3

Newspaper ($K)

Newspaper advertising budget per market. Range: 0.3 – 114.0 $K

Expected Output

The model returns a continuous numeric value representing predicted sales:

Low — below 8K units Moderate — 8–14K units Good — 14–20K units Excellent — 20K+ units

Real-World Relevance

This model enables smarter decisions in marketing budget allocation, sales forecasting, and ROI optimization across advertising channels.

Reading the Graphs

Feature plots: Each scatter shows real market observations. The regression line captures the linear trend for that channel. A tighter scatter around the line signals a stronger linear relationship.

Actual vs Predicted: Points along the dashed diagonal represent perfect predictions. Vertical distance from the diagonal is the prediction error. A well-calibrated model shows no systematic over- or under-prediction.
