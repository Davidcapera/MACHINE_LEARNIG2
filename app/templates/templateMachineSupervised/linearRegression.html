Linear Regression · Advertising Dataset

Predicting product sales from TV, Radio and Newspaper advertising budgets using Ordinary Least Squares Multiple Linear Regression.

1 · Problem Description

A company wants to understand which advertising channels drive the most sales and by how much, so the marketing team can allocate budgets efficiently and forecast revenue.

We apply Multiple Linear Regression to model the relationship between advertising spend (in thousands of dollars) across three media channels — TV, Radio and Newspaper — and the resulting sales volume (thousands of units). The learned equation allows predictions for any combination of budget inputs.

2 · Dataset Explanation

The Advertising dataset (James, Witten, Hastie & Tibshirani — ISLR) contains 200 market observations. Each row represents one market, recording how much was invested in three advertising channels and the resulting product sales. The dataset contains no missing values.

5 · Regression Graphs
Feature vs Sales — Regression Lines

Each subplot varies one feature while holding the others at their mean. The white line is the model's regression prediction.

Current vs. Predicted (Test Set)
Reading the Graphs

Feature plots: Each scatter shows real market observations. The regression line captures the linear trend for that channel. A tighter scatter around the line signals a stronger linear relationship. Radio shows the steepest slope — it has the highest marginal effect on sales.

Current vs Predicted: Points along the dashed diagonal represent perfect predictions. Vertical distance from the diagonal is the prediction error. A well-calibrated model shows no systematic over- or under-prediction across the entire sales range.
